Overview
In this project, you will design, implement, and evaluate a small-scale generative AI system using the generative modeling techniques you developed in your Nanodegree. You will choose a generative task and dataset, build and run a generative model, examine the quality and behavior of the generated outputs, and reflect on ethical considerations such as bias, misuse, or creative ownership.

This project uses only the tools you already know:

Python
NumPy
Pandas
PyTorch
Matplotlib / Seaborn
Jupyter Notebooks
You are building and running generative models in this project. The focus is on correct implementation, thoughtful evaluation of generated outputs, and responsible reasoning rather than producing polished or production-ready generations.

Why This Project Matters
Generative AI systems introduce unique technical and ethical challenges beyond traditional predictive modeling. Employers expect professionals to:

Implement generative models correctly using established architectures
Understand how training data influences generated outputs
Evaluate output quality using appropriate qualitative and quantitative approaches
Recognize risks related to bias, misuse, hallucination, or creative ownership
Communicate limitations and responsible-use considerations clearly
Connect technical design decisions to ethical implications
This project develops the applied judgment required to work responsibly with generative systems and prepares you for advanced generative and agentic AI work later in the capstone.

What You Will Build
You will create:

A Jupyter Notebook (generative_ai.ipynb) that:
loads and preprocesses a dataset appropriate for generative modeling
implements and runs a generative model (GAN, VAE, diffusion model, or Transformer-based text or image generation)
generates sample outputs from the trained model
includes visualizations or examples that support evaluation of output quality
documents key design choices and observations using Markdown cells
A Generative AI Analysis and Ethics Report (module_summary.pdf) that explains:
the generative task and why it is appropriate for the chosen model
the dataset and how it was prepared
the model architecture and training approach
how output quality was evaluated
ethical considerations related to bias, misuse, or creative ownership
limitations, risks, and potential improvements
This report must include in-text citations and a References section.
Citation Expectations
In your Generative AI Analysis and Ethics Report, you must use citations when you:

refer to generative modeling concepts or definitions (for example: GANs, VAEs, diffusion, attention mechanisms, or sampling strategies)
justify why a particular generative model or architecture is appropriate for your task
discuss evaluation practices for generative outputs
address ethical considerations, bias, misuse, or intellectual property concerns
You do not need to cite your own code, generated samples, plots, or observations that come directly from your notebook outputs. Your report must end with a References section listing all sources used, such as course materials, textbooks, or official framework documentation.

Academic Integrity Expectations
This project is designed to require authentic, individual work:

Generated outputs must come from your own model runs and dataset.
Your analysis must reference specific examples from your generated outputs.
Ethical reflection must be directly tied to your model, data, and task context.
Design decisions must be justified based on your actual implementation and results.
Generic descriptions or ethical statements not grounded in your work will not meet project expectations.
